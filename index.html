Captive 101

What is Captive Insurance?

A specialized form of self-insurance that empowers businesses to take a proactive approach to managing risk.

Specialized Form of Self-Insurance

Captive insurance companies are owned and controlled by the organizations they insure, providing a tailored approach to risk management.

Greater Control and Flexibility

Captive insurance allows businesses to have more control over their insurance policies, coverage, and risk management strategies.

Customized Risk Solutions

Captive insurance enables businesses to design insurance coverage that specifically addresses their unique risk exposures and requirements.

Captive insurance empowers businesses to take a proactive and strategic approach to managing their risks, tailored to their specific needs and goals.

Captive Insurance Structure

Aurora guides clients through every phase of captive insurance.

Aurora guides clients through every step of captive formation—from strategic planning and tailored domicile selection to full regulatory compliance. Our process includes robust AML procedures, ensuring a seamless and compliant path to establishing a captive that supports long-term success.

Captives are usually owned in a mirror image of the operating business. Ownership may be held by individuals, spouses, families, trusts, LLCs, partnerships, corporations, or other U.S. persons, tailored to meet client objectives.

Each jurisdiction has its own regulatory environment and ongoing compliance requirements, making careful selection essential to align with a client’s objectives. Aurora helps clients navigate these considerations to ensure the optimal domicile for long-term stability and effectiveness.

Our team oversees underwriting, and claims management while maintaining strict regulatory compliance. We handle accounting, reporting, and ongoing governance, giving clients confidence that their captive is managed with precision, efficiency, and transparency.

Aurora Risk Technologies manages all third-party requirements for captive ownership, ensuring full compliance and seamless coordination with regulators, auditors, and service providers. In addition, we offer clients access to a homogenous risk pool, creating greater stability, diversification, and financial strength within their captive programs.

Market Trends

Commercial Insurance Market Trends

The commercial insurance market is shifting. Here's why businesses are turning to captive solutions.

Rates Under Upward Pressure

In Q2 2025, U.S. commercial lines premiums increased ~3.8% year-over-year, though that growth has moderated from prior quarters.

Some Lines Harder Hit

Excess/umbrella liability, commercial auto, and general liability are seeing sharper increases, while property and cyber have shown signs of stabilization.

Rising Claim Costs

Inflation, rising materials and labor costs, and increased catastrophe (CAT) exposure pressure underwriters to raise rates to maintain profitability.

Market Volatility

Carriers tighten underwriting, raise deductibles, exclude or limit coverage for emerging or unquantifiable risks, and demand more stringent terms.

Coverage Constraints

Coverage may be constrained or unavailable in the traditional market, especially for unique, emerging, or hard-to-insure exposures.
